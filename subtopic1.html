Identifying Phishing Links and Red Flags

Source 1:

URL: ID Agent - Key Indicators of Phishing
Site Author: ID Agent
Abstract: This article by ID Agent helps readers recognize key signs of phishing messages, especially in emails. It explains how phishers use urgency, fake email addresses, poor grammar, and suspicious links to trick people. The article also discusses how phishers impersonate trusted brands or coworkers to get you to click a link or share information. It offers examples of real phishing attempts and explains how to hover over links to check if they're safe. It’s a simple and useful guide for anyone who wants to become better at spotting the early warning signs of a phishing attack.

Source 2:

URL: IPQualityScore - Malicious URL Scanner
Site Author: IPQualityScore
Abstract: IPQualityScore provides a free tool that scans suspicious URLs to detect phishing, malware, and fraud. Their article explains how phishing websites often imitate real companies using similar-looking domains or hidden redirects. The scanner uses machine learning to analyze links and content in real time—even for brand-new phishing sites. It also detects parked domains, command and control URLs, and malicious code embedded in websites. The site is helpful for cybersecurity teams or individuals who want to test a URL before visiting it. The article highlights how fast, smart scanning tools can reduce the chances of falling for a phishing link.
